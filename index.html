Stop Food Waste at UIUC

Every year, college dining halls waste millions of pounds of food. At UIUC, we can make a difference. Small changes in how we approach meals can reduce waste, save resources, and protect our environment. Your choices matter.

Why It Matters

40%

of food in the U.S. goes uneaten, with college dining halls contributing significantly to this waste.

8%

of global greenhouse gas emissions come from food waste. Reducing waste helps fight climate change.

25%

of all freshwater used in the U.S. goes to producing food that never gets eaten.

$218B

is the annual cost of food waste in America. Every plate saved helps reduce this economic burden.

What Works

UC San Diego Trayless Dining

UC San Diego removed trays from their dining halls, leading students to take only what they could carry. This simple change reduced food waste by 30% and saved thousands of gallons of water from tray washing.

30% reduction in food waste

Menus of Change Research

The Menus of Change University Research Collaborative found that smaller portion sizes and "sample before you serve" stations significantly reduce waste. Students who try before taking full portions waste 25% less food.

25% less waste with sampling stations
