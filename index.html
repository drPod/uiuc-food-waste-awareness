What Works

Real solutions from universities across the country show that small changes create big impact.

UC San Diego Trayless Dining

UC San Diego removed trays from their dining halls, leading students to take only what they could carry. This simple change reduced food waste by 25-30% per person and saved thousands of gallons of water from reduced tray washing.

"Trayless days saw a 25 to 30 percent reduction in food waste per person. The institutions generated 11,505 fewer pounds of food waste on trayless days."
25-30% reduction in food waste per person
↓ 25%

Menus of Change Research

The Menus of Change University Research Collaborative found that smaller portion sizes and "sample before you serve" stations significantly reduce waste. Students who try before taking full portions waste 25% less food.

"Students who sample food before taking full portions waste significantly less. This approach empowers students to make informed choices."
25% less waste with sampling stations
